Instructions

Step 1

Boil a kettle Peel and finely dice the onion(s) Peel and finely dice the carrot(s) Peel and dice the potatoes into approx. 2cm cubes



Step 2

Heat a large pan (with a matching lid) with 2 tbsp (4 tbsp) olive oil When hot, add the onion, carrot and potatoes with a pinch of salt Cook on a high heat for 5 min or until starting to colour and caramelise



Step 3

Add 300ml (450ml) of boiled water to a separate pot Add 1/2 tbsp (1 tbsp) of salt and the bay leaf(ves) Bring to a simmer on a medium low heat and carefully add the smoked fish Cover with a lid and cook for 4 min, or until cooked through (the fish is cooked once it appears opaque and flakes apart easily)



Step 4

Add 2 tbsp (3 tbsp) of flour to the caramelised vegetables and cook for 2 min further, stirring well to combine Strain the fish, keeping the water (this is your fish stock) Add the strained stock to the vegetables Transfer the cooked fish to your serving bowls, ready for step 7



Step 5

Bring the vegetables and stock to the boil Once boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer, cover with a lid and cook for 8 min or until the potatoes are cooked through



Step 6

Chop the parsley finely Measure out 300ml (450ml) of milk



Step 7

Once the potatoes are cooked, remove the lid and add the sweetcorn (no need to drain) and the milk Cook for 4 min on a very low heat, stirring constantly until warmed through and thickened Remove from the heat, stir in 1/2 of the chopped parsley Tip: Add more water if the mixture becomes too thick



Step 8

Ladle the chowder over the fish Finish with the remaining chopped parsley and a grind of black pepper Enjoy!
