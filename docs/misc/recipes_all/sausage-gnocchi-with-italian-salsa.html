Sausage Gnocchi With Italian Salsa

Description: We've got a technique for cooking sausages that guarantees speed and succulence. Gnocchi takes practically no time to cook, so all that remains is for you to make your fresh tomato and black olive salsa. This baby can be gnocchi-ed up in 20 minutes!

View on Gousto

Ingredients

  • 2 tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 400g gnocchi
  • 4 Cumberland sausages
  • 30g black olives
  • 10g fresh basil
  • 15g capers
  • 2 garlic cloves


Instructions



Step 1

Boil a kettle Heat a large, wide-based pan (preferably non-stick) with a matching lid over medium-high heat Add the sausages and cook for 6 min turning once midway through





Step 2

Meanwhile, dice the tomatoes Finely chop the basil leaves and stalks, keeping them separate (save a few leaves for garnish) Chop the black olives roughly Peel and finely chop (or grate) the garlic





Step 3

Add the gnocchi to a pot of salted boiling water over a high heat Cook for 4 min or until they all float to the top Once cooked, drain the gnocchi and return to the pot and keep covered until step 7





Step 4

Meanwhile, transfer the sausages to a chopping board and slice in half lengthways Tip: you can hold them with tongs if they're too hot to handle!





Step 5

Return the pan to a high heat and add the sausages cut-side-down  Cook for a further 2 min  Add 50ml (75ml) water, cover and cook for a further 4 min Once done, remove from the heat and keep covered until serving 





Step 6

Mix together the tomatoes, chopped basil leaves, olives, capers, balsamic and 1 tbsp (2 tbsp) olive oil - this is your salsa Season with salt and pepper





Step 7

Add the garlic, chopped basil stalks and 1 tbsp (2 tbsp) olive oil to the drained gnocchi and mix through gently



Step 8

Serve the gnocchi with the sausages and top with your salsa Tear the reserved basil leaves and scatter them over Enjoy!
