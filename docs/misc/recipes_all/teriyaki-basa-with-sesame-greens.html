Teriyaki Basa With Sesame Greens

Description: For this easy cook's trick, you'll season your basa in soy, honey and chilli to make a quick teriyaki glaze. Round out your meal with microwave rice and sesame greens to serve. Ingredients

  • Cooked white long grain rice (280g)
  • Basa fillets (2pcs)
  • Spring onion x2
  • Dried chilli flakes (0.5tsp)
  • Basa fillet x0
  • Mangetout (80g) x2
  • Soy sauce (30ml)
  • Toasted sesame seeds (5g)
  • Soy sauce (15ml) x0
  • Honey (25g)
  • Toasted sesame oil (10ml)


Instructions



Step 1

Before you begin... Get all your ingredients and equipment ready Skim through these steps, then wash your fruit and veg Note: Equipment and pan sizes vary. Make sure you cook food thoroughly (even if it takes you a little longer than 10 min)





Step 2

Now, let's get started! Boil a kettle Trim, then slice your spring onion[s] finely Tip: Cooking for 2 or more? Chop them in one bunch for speed!





Step 3

Add your mangetout to a bowl and cover them in boiled water until fully submerged Set aside to soften Combine your soy sauce, honey and chilli flakes (can't handle the heat? Go easy!) in a bowl – this is your teriyaki sauce





Step 4

Heat a large, wide-based pan (preferably non-stick) with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a medium-high heat Once hot, add your basa fillet[s] to the pan and cook for an initial 4 min on each side





Step 5

While the basa is cooking, squeeze your pouch[es] of cooked white long grain rice to separate the grains Tear the top corner of the pouch[es] (just a little!) and put the pouch in the microwave for 2 min or until piping hot Tip: If you're cooking two or more packets, pop them in together but increase the microwave cook time accordingly





Step 6

Add the teriyaki sauce and half the sliced spring onion (save some for garnish!) to the pan Cook for 1-2 min further on each side or until the basa is cooked through and the sauce has thickened slightly – this is your teriyaki basa Tip: Your fish is cooked once it turns opaque and flakes easily





Step 7

Once the sauce has thickened, drain the softened mangetout and return to the bowl Add your toasted sesame oil and toasted sesame seeds and give everything a good mix up – these are your sesame greens



Step 8

Serve the teriyaki basa with the cooked rice and sesame greens on the side Garnish with the remaining spring onion Enjoy!
