Chickpea Tikka Masala With Fragrant Rice

Description: Veggie or not, this plant-based tikka masala is a must-try. Serve the mild yet richly spiced sauce on a bed of fragrant rice, with fresh coriander and a sprinkle of almonds for crunch. Delish!

  • Solid creamed coconut (25g)
  • Mango chutney (20g) x2
  • White basmati rice (130g)

  • Garlic clove x2
  • Tomato paste (32g)
  • Star anise
  • Tomato x2
  • Brown onion x2
  • Flaked almonds (15g)


Instructions



Step 1

Add your basmati rice, star anise and 300ml [390ml] [600ml] cold water to a pot with a lid and bring to the boil over a high heat Once boiling, reduce the heat to very low and cook, covered, for 10-12 min or until all the water has absorbed and the rice is cooked Once cooked, remove from the heat and keep covered until serving – this is your fragrant rice





Step 2

While the rice is cooking, peel and finely chop your brown onion[s] Heat a large, wide-based pan (preferably non-stick) with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a medium heat Once hot, add the chopped onion and cook for 6-8 min or until soft





Step 3

Boil a kettle Drain and rinse your chickpeas Chop your tomato[es] finely Peel and finely chop (or grate) your garlic





Step 4

Once the onion has softened, add the chopped tomato and chopped garlic to the pan and cook for 4-5 min further





Step 5

Meanwhile, chop your creamed coconut roughly (if required!) Dissolve your vegetable stock mix, chopped creamed coconut and tomato paste in 300ml [400ml] [500ml] boiled water – this is your coconut stock Add your ground paprika, curry powder and drained chickpeas to the pan and cook for 1-2 min further





Step 6

Add the coconut stock and mango chutney to the pan and cook for 6-7 min further or until it's thickened in consistency – this is your chickpea tikka masala





Step 7

Chop your coriander finely, including the stalks



Step 8

Serve the chickpea tikka masala with the fragrant rice to the side (discard the star anise!) Sprinkle over your flaked almonds and chopped coriander Enjoy!
