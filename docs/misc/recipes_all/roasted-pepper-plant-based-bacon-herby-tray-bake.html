Roasted Pepper & Plant-Based Bacon Herby Tray Bake

Description: A plant-based tray bake that’s packed with delectable veg, and jives well with meat-free bacon and chive. You’ll roast potato, pepper and tomato, before topping with vegan lardons and tangy dressing. Ingredients

  • Spring greens (150g)
  • Chives (5g) x2
  • White wine vinegar (15ml)
  • Meat-free bacon lardons (60g)
  • Red pepper
  • Tomato x2
  • White potato x4


Instructions



Step 1

Preheat the oven to 220°C/ 200°C (fan)/ gas 7 Deseed your pepper[s] (scrape the seeds and pith out with a teaspoon) and chop into large pieces Cut your tomato[es] into wedges Chop your potatoes (skins on) into bite-sized pieces





Step 2

Add the chopped potatoes, chopped pepper and tomato wedges to a baking tray (or two!) with a drizzle of olive oil and a generous pinch of salt and pepper Give everything a good mix up and put the tray[s] in the oven for an initial 25-30 min or until everything is cooked through and starting to crisp





Step 3

Meanwhile, chop your chives roughly





Step 4

Add the chopped chives to a food processor with your white wine vinegar, 2 tbsp [3 tbsp] [4 tbsp] olive oil, 1 tsp [1 1/2 tsp] [2 tsp] sugar, a pinch of salt and pepper and 1 tbsp [1 1/2 tbsp] [2 tbsp] of water Blitz until smooth – this is your chive dressing





Step 5

Wash your spring greens then pat dry with kitchen paper Rip the leaves off the spring greens, discard the tough stalks, then layer the leaves over each other, roll them up and slice into thin strips





Step 6

Once the vegetables have had an initial 25-30 min, remove the tray from the oven and add your meat-free bacon lardons and the sliced spring greens and stir it all together





Step 7

Return the tray to the oven for a further 7 min or until the spring greens have wilted and the meat-free bacon is warmed through – this is your roasted pepper & plant-based bacon tray bake



Step 8

Serve the roasted pepper & plant-based bacon tray bake Drizzle the chive dressing all over and season with a generous grind of black pepper Enjoy!
