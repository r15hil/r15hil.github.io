Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp dried dill
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 150g bulgur wheat
  • 1 chicken stock cube
  • 4 British chicken thigh fillets
  • 30g walnuts
  • 2 tbsp French Dijon mustard
  • 2 spring onions
  • 1 celery stick
  • 100g seedless grapes


Instructions



Step 1

Preheat the oven to 200°C/ 180°C (fan)/ 400°F/ Gas 6 Boil a kettle





Step 2

Combine 2/3 of the Dijon mustard with 1 tbsp [2 tbsp] olive oil and the dried dill in a mixing bowl Add the chicken thigh fillets and coat evenly in the mixture Season generously with salt and pepper





Step 3

Add the chicken thighs to an oven tray (use tin foil to avoid mess) Put them in the oven for 25 min or until cooked through





Step 4

Meanwhile, dissolve the chicken stock cube[s] in a pot, with a matching lid, with 275ml [550ml] boiled water Add the bulgur wheat, cover and cook for 5 min or until almost all the water is absorbed Once done, cover and set aside for 10 min





Step 5

Meanwhile, cut the red seedless grapes in half Chop the spring onions finely, discarding the root ends Slice the celery stick[s] finely 





Step 6

Combine the remaining Dijon in a small bowl with the apple cider vinegar, 2 tbsp [3 tbsp] olive oil and a pinch of salt and pepper - this is your Dijon dressing Bash the walnuts (in the bag with a rolling pin) Add them to a small heatproof dish and place in the oven for 4 min or until slightly darkened 





Step 7

Once the chicken is cooked, transfer it to a clean board and cut into thin strips Pour any tray juices into the bulgur wheat Fluff the bulgur wheat with a fork and stir through the grapes, sliced celery, chopped spring onion, toasted walnuts and chicken strips - this is your chicken & Waldorf bulgur



Step 8

Serve the chicken & Waldorf bulgur with the Dijon dressing drizzled over Enjoy!
