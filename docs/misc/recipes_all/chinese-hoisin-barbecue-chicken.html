Chinese Hoisin Barbecue Chicken

Description: Savour The Summer with this sweet, umami-rich hoisin chicken, with added fresh plums for fruitiness. We like to think of it as Chinese barbecue chicken. Served with charred, nutrient-packed pak choi and some steamed sesame rice. It's tasty and (hoi)sinless! If you're feeling the heat, why not try cooking the chicken on the barbecue...

Ingredients

  • 250g pak choi
  • 2 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 150g fragrant basmati rice
  • 2 plums
  • 10g toasted sesame seeds
  • 4 British chicken thigh fillets
  • 1/2 tsp fiery chilli flakes
  • 2 spring onions
  • 2 soy sauce sachets (16ml)
  • 2 tbsp hoisin sauce


Instructions



Step 1

Preheat the oven to 220°C/ 200°C (fan)/ 425°F/ Gas 7 and season the chicken with the chilli flakes (can’t handle the heat? Go easy!) and a generous pinch of salt Heat a large, wide-based pan (with a matching lid) over a medium-high heat with 2 tbsp [4 tbsp] vegetable oil Once hot, cook the chicken for 2 min or until golden brown





Step 2

Meanwhile, slice the pak choi in half, lengthways Transfer the golden chicken to a baking tray (use tin foil to avoid mess!) and put it in the oven for 12-15 min  Return the pan to a high heat and once hot, add the pak choi, cut-side down and cook for 2-3 min or until charred  Once done, transfer the pak choi to a plate and reserve the pan





Step 3

Add the rice, 350ml [700ml] water and a pinch of salt to a pot with a lid over a high heat and bring to the boil Once boiling, reduce the heat to very low and cook, covered, for 10-15 min or until all the water has absorbed  





Step 4

Meanwhile, dice the plums finely, discarding the stones Return the pan to a medium-low heat and once hot, add the plums with 100ml [200ml] water Cook for 2-4 min





Step 5

Add the hoisin, soy sauce, sesame seeds and 1 tsp [2 tsp] sugar and cook for 1-2 min or until the sauce has a thin jam-like consistency Once done, remove from the heat and stir in half the toasted sesame oil - this is your hoisin plum sauce





Step 6

Remove the chicken thighs from the oven and push them to one side of the tray Spoon a little of the hoisin-plum sauce over each chicken thigh, add the pak choi to the same tray and return the tray to the oven for a final 5 min  





Step 7

Meanwhile, slice the spring onion finely, discarding the root ends Once cooked, fluff the rice with a fork and stir in the spring onion and remaining toasted sesame oil



Step 8

Serve the chicken over the rice, with the pak choi to the side Drizzle with the remaining sauce  Enjoy!
