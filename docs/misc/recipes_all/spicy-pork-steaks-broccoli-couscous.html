Spicy Pork Steaks & Broccoli Couscous

Description: Harissa is sometimes described as the main condiment of Tunisia. Here, this North African flavour meets a warm couscous, broccoli and apple pilaf. Throw in some thyme and top with a succulent charred pork steak and you've got something truly harisspecial!

View on Gousto

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp harissa paste
  • 2 British pork neck steaks
  • 1 apple
  • 5g fresh thyme
  • 120g couscous
  • 1 broccoli
  • 30g dried cranberries


Instructions



Step 1

Boil a kettle Chop the thyme and leaves very finely, throwing away any woody parts Pat the pork steaks dry with kitchen towel and season both sides generously with salt and pepper Coat the steaks evenly on both sides with the harissa paste 





Step 2

Add the couscous and thyme leaves to a heatproof bowl with 225ml [450ml] boiled water and a pinch of salt and pepper, cover and set aside until later  





Step 3

Heat a large, wide-based pan (preferably non-stick) with a matching lid, with 2 tbsp [4 tbsp] vegetable oil over a high heat Once hot, add the steaks, cover with a lid and cook for 4 min (without turning) until nicely charred





Step 4

Meanwhile, cut the broccoli into small florets, discarding the stems Chop the dried cranberries roughly Grate the apple[s], (skin on) discarding the core[s]





Step 5

Turn the pork Add the broccoli to the pan  Cover and cook for a further 5 min or until the pork is cooked through





Step 6

Once done, transfer the pork to a plate and set it aside to rest Return the pan to a medium heat Add the couscous, cranberries and apple with 50ml [75ml] boiled water Stir to combine





Step 7

Slice the pork as finely as you can (against the grain)



Step 8

Serve the sliced pork over the couscous, with any resting juices spooned over Enjoy!
