One Pot Bacon Pasta

Description: The secret to this dish is cooking your linguine in the same pan as you've fried your bacon, before adding in a measured amount of stock. You then throw in your raw ingredients: garlic, cherry tomatoes, cheese and herbs, to cook as one; marrying together to form perfectly cooked pasta and a silky smooth sauce.

Ingredients

  • 40g Irish mature cheddar
  • 125g cherry tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp Italian dried oregano
  • 200g linguine
  • 1 chicken stock cube
  • 135g British dry cured smoked streaky bacon
  • 1/2 tsp fiery chilli flakes
  • 10g fresh basil
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 onion


Instructions



Step 1

Boil a kettle Chop the bacon finely  Heat a large, dry pan with a lid over a medium heat  Once hot, add the bacon and cook for 3-5 min until crispy 





Step 2

Add 700ml [1.4L] boiled water to the bacon and crumble in the stock cube[s] Whisk to dissolve the stock cube[s] and bring to the boil





Step 3

Meanwhile, chop the cherry tomatoes in half Grate the cheddar Peel and finely slice the garlic Peel and slice the onion[s] finely  





Step 4

Once the stock is boiling, reduce the heat to medium and add the linguine (break the linguine in half if it doesn't fit) Add the tomatoes, onion, garlic, dried oregano, chilli flakes (can't handle the heat? Go easy!) and half the grated cheddar to the pan





Step 5

Add 2 tbsp [4 tbsp] olive oil, 1 tsp [2 tsp] sugar and 1 tsp [2 tsp] pepper to the pot Cover with a lid and cook for 5 min





Step 6

After 5 min, remove the lid, and stir continuously until the pasta is cooked and a sauce has formed (add more water if it looks too dry) Tip: stirring the sauce releases the starch from the pasta making it naturally creamier





Step 7

Meanwhile, chop the basil finely, including the stalks



Step 8

Once the linguine is cooked, remove from the heat and stir in the basil and remaining cheddar Serve with a good grind of pepper  Enjoy!
